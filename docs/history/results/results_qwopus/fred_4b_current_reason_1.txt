## Solution

### Step 1: Calculate total work remaining
The backlog has **200 story points** of work remaining.

### Step 2: Determine required velocity per sprint
To finish exactly 200 points in 3 sprints, the team needs to complete $ \frac{200}{3} = 66.\overline{6} $ points per sprint on average. Since story point velocities are typically whole numbers (and you can't do partial points), we need **at least 67 points per sprint**.

### Step 3: Calculate current velocity
The team's actual velocity over the last 3 sprints was:
- Sprint 1: 35 points
- Sprint 2: 42 points  
- Sprint 3: 38 points

**Current average:** $ \frac{35 + 42 + 38}{3} = \frac{115}{3} = 38.\overline{3} $ ≈ **38.3 points per sprint** (as given)

### Step 4: Calculate the difference
**Required velocity:** 67 points/sprint  
**Current average:** ~38.3 points/sprint  

**Extra needed per sprint:** $ 67 - 38.3 = \boxed{28.7} $ **points per sprint**
